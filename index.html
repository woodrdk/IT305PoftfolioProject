As a developer I use my technical talent to help people bring their creative and technical ideas to life.

My computer history started with taking some continuing education computer programming classes back in 1997 with my grandfather. I then started using my technical learning at my job doing their website and making websites for some other small businesses. After high school I shifted career directions following my love of automotive and became an automotive mechanic eventually becoming a bus mechanic up until 2014. After having a motorcycle accident leaving me unable to do mechanic work I began to think about new careers. Receiving a laptop for christmas in 2016 and seeing and ad for a class about making Android apps popped up drawing my interest. So after that freebie class I started looking into a formal education in the field of computer programming. Seven months later July 5th, 2017 to be exact I started classes at Clover Park Technical College. I took classes in the study of Computer Programming program, completing both the AAS-T and AAS degrees offerred by CPTC at the same time, in 7 Qtrs. I finished this program March 21, 2019 and am enrolled with plans to start at Green River College in September 2019 for their BAS in Software Development.

Previous clients I have done work for include Casey's Auction Inc, MicroPower Plus, Nomad Computer Services.

My paralysis has created a more technical drive of projects for both a personal level and for the disabled community. This has helped me come up with several ideas including a current project I am working on www.whowherehow.org (still in beta), along with other things like learning more of the programming arduino, and raspberry pi computers to make devices to assist the disabled. A couple personal projects I have in the works would be wheelchair assisted drive, remote control grocery hauler, and home automation on a diy level,+ to name a few projects.
